  2. league
    [l]
    NOUN
    leagues (plural noun)
    1. a collection of people, countries, or groups that combine for a particular purpose, typically mutual protection or cooperation:
      "the League of Nations"
      synonyms: alliance · confederation · confederacy · federation ·
      [more]
      union · association · coalition · consortium · affiliation · guild · cooperative · partnership · fellowship · syndicate
      • an agreement to combine for a particular purpose.
    2. a group of sports clubs that play each other over a period for a championship.
      • the contest for the championship of a league:
        "the year we won the league"
    3. a class or category of quality or excellence:
      "the two men were not in the same league" ·
      [more]
      "Jack's in a league of his own"
      synonyms: class · group · circle · category · level
    VERB
    leagues (third person present) · leagued (past tense) · leagued (past participle) · leaguing (present participle)
    1. join in a league or alliance:
      "Oscar had leagued with other construction firms"
      synonyms: ally · join forces · join together · unite · band together ·
      [more]
      affiliate · combine · amalgamate · confederate · team up · join up
    ORIGIN
    late Middle English (denoting a compact for mutual protection or advantage): via French from Italian lega, from legare to bind, from Latin ligare.
